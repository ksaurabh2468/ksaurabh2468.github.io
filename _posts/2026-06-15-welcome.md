For most of my career, my work has lived inside companies — in program plans, decks,
and systems that ship behind a login. This space is the opposite: a place that's
genuinely mine, where I can think out loud about the work I care about.

So, hello. I'm **Kumar Saurabh** — a PMP-certified program leader who spends his days
turning AI ideas into products that actually make it to production.

## What you'll find here

I want this blog to be useful, not promotional. A few themes I'll keep coming back to:

- **Delivering AI for real** — the gap between an impressive proof-of-concept and a system
  that survives contact with production, and how to close it.
- **Program leadership** — stakeholder alignment, scoping, and the unglamorous discipline
  that separates shipped from stalled.
- **Notes from the field** — computer vision, generative & agentic AI, and the patterns
  I see repeating across projects.

## How I think about the work

> The hardest part of an AI project is rarely the model. It's everything around it —
> the data, the stakeholders, the change management, the question of whether anyone
> will actually use it.

If there's one belief that shapes how I lead, it's that. The technology is necessary
but never sufficient. The win comes from connecting it to a real decision someone makes.
